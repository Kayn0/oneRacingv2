As the manager of your own Formula One race team, with a starting budget you need to purchase a driver, chassis and power unit for both of your cars. After each F1 race weekend your drivers, chassis and power units will recieve points based on the race results. In addition the value of each part will rise or fall depending on its performance in the race.

Just before the start of each Formula One race weekend a lockout will be established, no longer allowing trades or upgrades.

Please note:
If either of your cars are missing a part (driver, chassis, power unit), then no points are scored for that car.

To maximise your score and rise in the rankings, you should always be looking to improve your team by making trades.

You start the season with twenty trades. Each time you swap a part, it will cost you one trade.

During your first round all trades are free, so experiment with different combinations of drivers, chassis and power units until you are happy with your team.

If you're running low on trades you can purchase additional trades, look for the + icon on the team page.

Don't forget
When you make a trade you recieve the value of the traded item.

Purchasing an upgrade will double that parts score in the next round.

Please note:
If you make an upgrade and then trade out that particular part, you will lose the upgrade!

Just before each race weekend the app will enter a lockout status. During this time you will not be able to make trades or upgrades until the race weekend is completed. The timer shows how long until the next lock out.

The points allocated to drivers, chassis and power units after each race weekend is calculated from a combination of results.

Drivers

Drivers points are calculated by their quallifying position, race result and how many positions were gained during the race.

Chassis

Chassis points are calculated by the position each chassis qualified and race result of both chassis.

Power Units

Lastly power unit points are calulated by the best qualifying result of each brand and their race result.

Once the Formula one race results have been released and pushed into the app the dollar values and scores of drivers, chassis, power units and player teams are final.

What happens if a driver is replaced?

If during the Formula One race season a driver is unable to drive and is replaced for a race, the replacment driver will race under the usual drivers alias. Any teams effected by this will automatically be awared the results of the replacment driver after the race.
